# UK Consumer Rights Act 2015

## Your Consumer Rights

Under the Consumer Rights Act 2015, you have strong protection when buying goods, services, or digital content.

## Goods Rights

When you buy goods, they must be:
- Of satisfactory quality
- Fit for purpose
- As described

## Return and Refund Rights

### First 30 Days
You have the right to return faulty goods for a full refund within 30 days of purchase.

### After 30 Days
You can request repair or replacement. If the repair or replacement fails or is not possible, you can then request a refund.

## Digital Content Rights

For digital content, you have the right to:
- A refund if the content is faulty and cannot be fixed
- Compensation if faulty digital content damages your device

## Services Rights

Services must be:
- Carried out with reasonable care and skill
- Completed within a reasonable time
- Charged at a reasonable price (if no price agreed in advance)

## Distance Selling

For purchases made online, by phone, or by mail order, you have:
- 14 days to cancel and return items (cooling-off period)
- Right to full refund within 14 days of cancellation
- Seller pays return costs if you were not informed of your right to cancel

## Exceptions

Some items cannot be returned, including:
- Perishable goods
- Personalized items
- Items that deteriorate quickly

## Getting Help

If a retailer refuses your valid claim:
- Use their complaints procedure
- Contact Trading Standards
- Consider Alternative Dispute Resolution
- Seek legal advice for valuable items
